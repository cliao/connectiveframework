Interconnection is a project that studies individual geographic ties and networking connections. The project began in February of 2013, and is a work in progress.


The simple question, “Where are you from?” can result in a number of valid responses from the same person. I presented this question to 10 people from three distinct networks whom I "know from Los Angeles" (the place I identify with the most.) I also ask them to include a photo of something they distinctly associate with the location. The idea is to see the different interpretations that people can have of the same place. Each person then refers me to another person they consider to also be from the same location, and presumably the chain goes on.


Through these channels of connections, I analyze where it takes me geographically, and how each person interprets the questions. It explores the who, what, and where people identify with, and the many ways they interconnect. I want to see how the locations and interpretations evolve, or don't evolve, and examine which channels thrive and where they break.
